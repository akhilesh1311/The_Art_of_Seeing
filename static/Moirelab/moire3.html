Optical illusions with dots

Moiré patterns are caused by interference of two or more layers adjusted slightly out of sync. First we create a layer of randomly generated dots, and create an exact the same layer stacked on top of it. The below visualization allows you to manipulate the two layers. You can set the two layers to different colors so that it is easier to see.

A small shoutout to Q science: Moiré Pattern, which explains the phenomenon.

Suggested Settings

Rotate the layers by a small amount (between -5 to 5 degrees). If you exceed this, the visual effect gets harder to see. Similarly, when you move the layers, small values (between -3 to 3) are best for seeing the visual effect.
